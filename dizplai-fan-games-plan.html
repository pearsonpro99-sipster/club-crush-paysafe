02 · Architecture

One Widget. One Web App Per Client.

The Fan Games suite runs as a standalone PWA embedded as a widget inside each club's dedicated Dizplai Mythos web app. No shared infrastructure between clubs — each has full isolation.

Why This Architecture

Complete club isolation

Villa fans never see Arsenal branding. Each instance is fully independently themed and deployed.

Reuses existing Mythos infrastructure

Same Bitbucket pipeline, same Docker/ECS setup, same auth layer already in the Mythos platform.

Games stay as Next.js + Phaser

Phaser 3 (canvas-based) doesn't SSR. Keeping games as a standalone Next.js PWA embedded via iframe is the correct technical approach — no rewrite required.

04 · Revenue Model

Three Commercial Streams

🏟️

Platform Licence

Monthly SaaS fee per club. Includes hosting, updates, one custom game skin, leaderboard and basic reporting.

£2,000–£6,000 / mo
🪙

Coin Rev-Share

Clubs white-label coin packs. Dizplai takes 20% of all in-app coin purchases. Club earns 80% — direct revenue from fans, zero effort.

20% of gross IAP
🎯

Sponsored Games

A partner (kit sponsor, bookmaker, beverage brand) funds a bespoke game or in-game campaign. Brand featured throughout.

£5,000–£20,000 / campaign

05 · Client Proposition

Why Clubs Buy This

📱 Captures the Second Screen

65% of fans use their phone during matches. Fan Games captures that attention with club-branded content — not a competitor's app.

🔁 Daily Habit, Not Just Matchday

Streaks and leaderboards drive fans back every single day. Engagement becomes habitual — not just 90 minutes on a Saturday.

💰 New Revenue With Zero Engineering

Clubs earn 80% of coin purchases. No development cost. No ongoing maintenance. Dizplai handles everything.

🎨 Genuinely Club-Branded

McGinn's goggles. Gunnersaurus. Club colours, chants, stadium backdrops. Fans feel it — this isn't generic gamification dropped into a white-label shell.

📊 First-Party Fan Data

Every session builds a rich fan profile — game preferences, spending, session times. Clubs own this data for commercial partnerships and sponsorship proposals.

⚡ Live in Weeks, Not Months

The POC is already built. Aston Villa could be live in production within 6–8 weeks of a signed agreement. No long procurement cycles needed.
